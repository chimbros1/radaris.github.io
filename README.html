RADARIS

Advanced Frontal Collision Warning System

Scientific Abstract

The Radaris project focuses on developing a hazard warning system for frontal collisions in limited visibility conditions. Current safety systems often depend on optical sensors which fail during fog or heavy rain. Our hypothesis is that a radio-frequency based system can provide reliable alerts regardless of weather, significantly reducing road accidents.

Research Objective

To create an affordable, easy-to-implement hardware solution that utilizes radio waves to alert drivers of hazards before they are visible to the human eye.

Technical Novelty

Unlike traditional ADAS, Radaris uses 2.4 GHz radio signals to detect obstacles and communicate between vehicles autonomously, ensuring terrain and weather independence.

Problem Scale and Statistics

Data from the World Health Organization (WHO) indicates that roughly 25% of all road accidents occur when visibility is poor. Statistics show a dangerous upward trend in fatalities as traffic density increases.

Technical Architecture

The hardware is built around the ATmega 328 microcontroller and NRF24L01 wireless modules. The system is designed to trigger visual and audio alerts when obstacles are detected within the transmission range.

Core Component Specifications

  • Microcontroller ATmega 328
  • Wireless Module NRF24L01 (2.4 GHz)
  • Operating Range Up to 900 Meters
  • Power Supply 5V / 9V DC
  • Alert Mechanism Buzzer & LCD Display

Antenna Engineering

The system uses a custom-designed antenna optimized for the ISM band to maintain signal integrity during heavy precipitation.

  • Antenna Material 1.0mm Copper Wire
  • Optimized Length 90.0 cm
